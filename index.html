One request, wrapped for delivery

You start with just the real data at the client — one pod calling another inside a Kubernetes cluster. Step forward with Next (or the arrow keys) and watch each layer wrap around it, down the client's stack, across the underlay, and back up the server's stack.

The bar shows the ratio — how little of what's finally on the wire is the thing you actually asked for. A GET or OPTIONS has no body at all, so it's pure envelope.

Tap any layer to see its real header fields, as tcpdump would show them. The rail up top tracks where you are on the path. Hit 🎲 New request for a fresh POST, GET, preflight and more.
